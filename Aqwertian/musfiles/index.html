Mus Files for musplay

Jim Plank

The musplay home page

You can ftp these files via anonymous ftp to cs.utk.edu, in the directory pub/plank/music/musfiles.
These are input files for the musplay program. I've divided these into the following difficulty levels:
  • Trivial: you should be able to play this perfectly the first one or two times.
  • Easy: After a few run-throughs, you should be able to play these perfectly.
  • Medium: You'll probably have to look at the music, go through it a few times, and maybe even practice a spot or two.
  • Getting Harder: You'll have to practice a bit, but it's not really that hard.
  • Hard: You'll have to practice quite a bit, and it is pretty hard.
  • Really Hard: Does not really fit this paradigm for one reason or another.
I need to give credit to Henri Casanova for entering a bunch of these. Thanks, Henri.

Trivial

Most of these are children's songs that my 4-year old daughter can play, but the Satie Gymnopedies fit in here too.
  • little-dog.mus: ``Oh Where, Oh Where Has My Little Dog Gone.''
  • bingo.mus: ``Bingo''.
  • brother-john.mus: ``Brother John''. (Are you sleeping?).
  • peter-cottontail.mus: ``Peter Cottontail''.
  • bibbidi.mus: ``Bibbidi Bobbidy Boo'' (from Cinderella).
  • farmer.mus: ``The Farmer In The Dell''.
  • how-great.mus: The hymn: ``How Great Thou Art''.
  • Satie-Gym-1.mus: Gymnopedie number 1, by Satie.
  • Satie-Gym-2.mus: Gymnopedie number 2, by Satie.
  • Satie-Gym-3.mus: Gymnopedie number 3, by Satie.

Easy

  • fur.mus: ``Fur Elise'', by Beethoven. Entered by Henri.
  • scarborough.mus: ``Scarborough Fair'', by Simon and Garfunkel.
  • yesterday.mus: ``Yesterday'', by the Beatles.
  • ch09-2.mus: Nocturne, Opus 9, number 2, by Chopin.
  • ch28-15.mus: Prelude, Opus 28, number 15, by Chopin. Known as the ``raindrop'' prelude. This one looks really cool while you're playing it.
  • bach-p6.mus: Prelude #6 by Bach. Entered by Henri.
  • scr22-1.mus: Prelude, Opus 22, number 1 by Scriabin. Good if you're feeling a little moody.
  • prok-vf01.mus: Vision Fugitive, Opus 22, number 1 by Prokofiev.
  • liszt-nuages.mus: ``Nuages Gris,'' by Liszt. Yes, you can sight-read the real thing, but this is even easier. The only drag is that the octave trills can't be played as octave trills, but you have to play them as regular trills. Someday I'll try to fix that.
  • golden-bells.mus: Hymn: ``When They Ring The Golden Bells.''
  • satie-yachting.mus: ``Le Yachting'', from ``Sports et Divertissements'', by Satie.
  • satie-colin.mus: ``Colin-Maillard'', from ``Sports et Divertissements'', by Satie.

Medium

  • Titanic.mus: ``My Heart Will Go On'' -- the Love Theme from the Titanic. Feeling like Liberace? There is no better tune. Actually, it's a pretty nice arrangement, and I'm sure I'm enfringing on copyrights here.
  • ipanema.mus: ``The Girl from Ipanema'' -- I'm not overly happy with the arrangement, but there are times in one's life when only Ipanema will do.
  • brahms-hd5.mus: Hugarian Dance #5 by Brahms. This should probably go into the ``getting harder'' section, but it's awfully fun to play.
  • pre.mus: Prelude to Cantos d'Espana by Albeniz (also known as Asturias by some).
  • rach23-5.mus: Prelude in G# minor, Opus 23, number 5 by Rachmaninoff. Thanks to Henri for putting this in. I think the middle section needs some work, but evidently not enough to actually go ahead and do it.
  • rach3-1.mus: ``Elegie'', Opus 3, number 1 by Rachmaninoff. A good one if you're feeling moody, and like pounding the keyboard a bit.
  • schm15-12.mus: ``Bingo''. Schumann's "child falling asleep" from Kinderszenen. Opus 15, number 12.
  • schu143-1.mus: First movement of Schubert's sonata in A minor, opus 143. Don't fall asleep playing this one!
  • ch18.mus: Grande Valse Brillante, Opus 18, by Chopin. Is this not the most perfect waltz ever written?
  • ch06-1.mus: Mazurka, Opus 6, number 1 by Chopin.
  • ch28-14.mus: Prelude, opus 28, number 14 by Chopin, entered by Henri. This is the one where the left and right hand are in unison, blasting away triplets. Can you make this piece sound like anything but noise???
  • ch28-22.mus: Prelude, opus 28, number 22, by Chopin, again entered by Henri.
  • ch40-1.mus: Polonaise, opus 40, number 1, by Chopin. Turn up the volume and hope the neighbors aren't home!
  • scr11-6.mus: Prelude, Opus 11, number 6 by Scriabin. Good and stormy. Entered by Henri.
  • prok-vf02.mus: Vision Fugitive, Opus 22, number 2 by Prokofiev. Rip those runs off!
  • prok-vf07.mus: Vision Fugitive, Opus 22, number 7 by Prokofiev.

Getting Harder

  • rav-ma.mus: ``Menuet Antique,'' by Ravel.
  • rav-tomb-forlane.mus: ``Forlane,'' from ``Le Tombeau de Couperin,'' by Ravel.
  • schm2.mus: Papillons, opus 2, by Schumann.
  • clair.mus: ``Clair de Lune,'' by Debussy. I probably need to give this some attention and make it easier to play.
  • liszt-hr3.mus: Hungarian Rhapsody #3 by Liszt.
  • rach39-4.mus: Etude Tableau, Opus 39, number 4 by Rachmaninoff.
  • alb-cordoba.mus: ``Cordoba''. from Cantos d'Espana, by Albeniz.
  • soler_R88-Db.mus: Sonata in Db, Rubio 88, by Antonio Soler. This is a fun one to watch while you are playing it.
  • prok12-8.mus: Allemande from Episodes, by Prokofiev. Opus 12, number 8. In the part where the left hand is supposed to cross over the right, I've set it up so you have to do that...

Hard

  • chb1.mus: Ballade number 1, opus 23, by Chopin.
  • chb3.mus: Ballade number 3, opus 47, by Chopin.
  • rach39-9.mus: Etude Tableau, Opus 39, number 9, by Rachmaninoff.
  • scar-C.mus: Sonata in C, by Scarlatti. This one shouldn't be tough, but I have problems playing it fast enough.

Really Hard

  • rach23-4.mus: Prelude, Opus 23, number 4 by Rachmaninoff. Hemoilas in one hand. I think I could get this if I practiced enough, but I haven't done so...
